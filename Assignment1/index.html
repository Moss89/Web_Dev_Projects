Japan and China Compared.

Both countries are well known, located in Asia, and are neighbours. But many people struggle to decide which country to visit first. Hopefully this post will help you decide!

The first thing to know about China is that it is HUGE. A trip to China is like a trip to Europe; you'll need to travel A LOT to see everything. So if you plan on taking in most of the famous sights in China, then you will need to have a lot of free time. Conversely, Japan is a very small country, and therefore more suited for short trips. To travel around China, flying is your fastest choice, but if you are sick of flights, the country operates many modern high speed trains, which are relatively cheap, and quite comfortable. In Japan, the high speed trains are the way to go. Flying could actually take longer due to the usual airport nonsense, and the views of the country whilst on the train are breathtaking.

As for the cost of the visit, flights to and from both countries should be in the same ballpark, but with China you have more cities as options, so you might find a bargain if you are flexible with your starting city. Food is generally cheaper in China than in Japan, with one important caveat: Western food in China is quite expensive, whereas it costs roughly the same as local food in Japan. So, as long as you can skip your burritos, China will be cheaper! However, it must be noted that food safety in China isn't always the highest concerrn, so if you aren't "adventurous" in this regard, Japan is a safe bet. If you like to go to the supermarket and cook for yourself, China is the more affordable of the two, but Japan has higher quality products.

To give an easier picture of how the prices compare, I created a handy little table. Green means affordable, yellow means reasonable, and red means pricey! But like I stated above, you often get what you pay for. So while China is definitely the cheaper country, if quality and comfort are your priority, then maybe Japan is the better option for you.
